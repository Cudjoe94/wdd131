Abia State, Nigeria.

Abia State, located in southeastern Nigeria, is known for its rich cultural heritage and economic activities. The capital city is Umuahia, while the commercial hub is Aba. Abia is predominantly inhabited by the Igbo ethnic group and is known for its vibrant traditional festivals, crafts, and local cuisine. The state's economy is driven by agriculture, with palm oil, cassava, and yams being major crops. Aba is famous for its bustling markets and industries, particularly in textiles and leather goods. Abia State also has several educational institutions and healthcare facilities, contributing to its development.
